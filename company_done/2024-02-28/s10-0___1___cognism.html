About us

Cognism is a leader in international sales intelligence, setting a new standard for data quality and compliance, trusted by 1800+ revenue teams worldwide. Cognism helps businesses find, engage and close their dream prospects by providing premium company and contact information, including firmographics, technographics, sales trigger events, intent data, verified business emails and phone-verified mobile numbers. Next level GDPR & CCPA compliance, combined with innovative technology and integrations with leading CRM and sales engagement partners, make Cognism the number one choice for businesses looking to create a predictable pipeline, find their next best business opportunity and overcome global compliance barriers. Following multiple successful funding rounds and the acquisition of Mailtastic (2020), an email signature solution provider, and Kaspr (2022), a Paris-based sales prospecting tool, there has never been a more exciting time to join us.       How do you nurture content leads when you dont gate your long-form content? Traditionally, eBooks and other long-form content would be hidden behind a gate, and the content would only be shared in exchange for an email address. This email address would then be added into a nurture sequence, trying to push this prospect through the funnel to buy. But we know buyer behaviour has changed - and buyers cant be pushed through a funnel. They buy when they decide they have a need to buy. Plus, at Cognism, we believe there are far more benefits to ungating your content and offering value upfront vs the friction that having a gate can add. This takes us back to the initial question - if youre not gating your long-form content, how can you nurture those people until theyre ready to come to in-market? Liam Bartholomew, Cognisms VP of Marketing says: Just because you dont have a tangible email address, lots of people think you cant nurture the audience. You can, its just not the traditional way. Instead, its about consistently delivering them value over time. Instead of emailing them, you can encourage those engaging with your long form content into your value loops. For example subscribing to your podcast or your newsletter. Somewhere else where they can regularly get value from you. That way, people keep coming back to your content - they almost nurture themselves. For more on this, check out episode 4 of this eBook mini series in the comments! #b2bmarketing

      In RevOps, youre often operating as a team of 1. This means prioritising your problems and remaining open-minded, but also not trying to change everything at once. Remember to pick your battles. Heres Anastasiia Binns's advice: When you start at a new company, everyone already has a to-do list for you. Some things are urgent, and some are issues that arent necessarily a priority. The trick is to rank each of these and identify them in a couple of quadrants: One is going to be the quick win, ones going to be a major project, and ones going to be a thankless task. Then, you can start to have conversations about which problems to tackle first. Go and ask people what the main problems theyre experiencing are. Then you normally have a list of quick wins that you can focus on to win the hearts and minds of people that you will be forcing change upon. Some changes can be fast, and some probably warrant a little bit of slow speed because you want to make sure everyones on board with you to adopt those changes. Thats the key to winning over your stakeholders and building trust. Looking for more advice? Check out the top takeaways from Anastasiia Binns and Jeff Ignacio on The RevOps Review!
