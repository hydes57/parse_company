    Nettxio attended Josh Loaiza's session at NATE Unite 2024, entitled “Unlocking the Power of Telecommunications: Equipping the Industry to Prevent Human Trafficking.” It was our first intro to TATE: Telecommunications Against Trafficking and Exploitation. We went in blind, not knowing for sure if Nettxio was positioned to be of assistance, with the assumption being made this was a tech-based solution to human trafficking. It was surprising, and honestly empowering, to learn the best defense in combating human trafficking is educating ourselves on signs to be aware of while going about our normal routines. How to spot a person who is being trafficked. And knowing how to report those signs. This is an arena we can all jump in and throw a hook. One of the biggest hurdles facing law enforcement is retraining us all to understand trafficking does not always look like what’s sensationalized on tv. People tied up in darkened, hidden rooms or stacked up in cargo containers arriving on ships – that’s not the scene. The presenting foundation, 3Strands Global Foundation, was founded in response to a 17-year-old girl in a tight-knit California community being trafficked for 8 days after befriending a 22-year-old at the park, before being recovered. It is a crime that thrives in plain sight. The FREE 30-minute RAN, Recognize and Notify, training is an easy first step to training our minds to look out for each other, to be more aware. We want to thank NATE, TATE and 3Strands Global Foundation for the presentation and their ongoing work. It was eye-opening and we’re proud to say we already have one Nettxio member who is RAN certified! TATE RAN training can be found at this link: https://lnkd.in/gVuaPDN National Human Trafficking Hotline: 1-888-373-7888 #NateUnite #endhumantrafficking
