About us

The Smart Learning Factory – Skopje (SFLS) was established in 2023 and situated at the Faculty of Mechanical Engineering, “Ss. Cyril and Methodius” University in Skopje. It is run by a team from the department of Industrial Engineering and Management. The SLFS originates from the Learn4SMEs project “Learning Factory for Improving Digital Competitiveness and Industry 4.0 Readiness of SMEs in Balkans” financed by EIT-Manufacturing, co-funded by the European Union where Technical University Wien and Festo Learn Zentrum guided the process. The main goal of the SFLS is to create a physical simulation environment for learning production concepts where students and people from the industry can learn new technologies and further develop their skills, focusing on Lean Management and Industry 4.0. For now, the SLFS is equipped with a Smart Kanban supermarket, smart Poka Yoke station, working station, transporter, 2d machine vision and a SCARA robot. The Smart Learning Factory – Skopje is envisioned as: - Showroom to discover new technologies for Lean and Industry 4.0 - Laboratory for experimenting with new technologies and scenarios - Training center for upskilling and reskilling future industry skills - Center for developing industry-specific solutions

    Update on our ongoing Lean Six Sigma training at the Smart Learning Factory - Skopje, Faculty of Mechanical Engineering at Ss.Cyril and Methodius University of Skopje! The first session of the training is finishing up, featuring four dynamic days led by Prof. Bojan Jovanoski, Prof. Robert Minovski, and Ass. Aleksandar Argilovski. Participants delved into Lean Six Sigma essentials and the DMAIC framework, exploring real-world applications within our learning factory.   Throughout the days, we had several partner visits that made this training even more exciting. In the beginning, we were honored to receive Christopher Dodge VD. USAID Director of the Office of Economic Development. In the later sessions, Treetech Solution and Smart Energy Solution added flair by presenting their state-of-art solutions in order to inspire our trainees. Finally, our partners from Rade Koncar TEP brought a global perspective, uniting professionals from Japan, Czechia, Hungary, Romania, and North Macedonia within our learning factory.   Now, participants return to their production corridors with a two-week window to apply their newfound Lean Six Sigma. Meanwhile, three more such sessions are planned for 2024 and registration is open for all interested companies at the following link: https://lnkd.in/d442k2tQ or visit our website learningfactory.mf.edu.mk These trainings are supported by USAID Partnerships for Economic Growth Activity and they will finance 50% of the total price of the training for the first 40 participants!

    Yesterday (16/11/2023) we had the privilege of hosting two captivating lectures by Professor Wilfried Sihn and Professor Fazel Ansari, both esteemed members of the Vienna University of Technology. The first lecture was held at the Faculty of Mechanical Engineering at Ss.Cyril and Methodius University of Skopje, and it was tailored for the academic staff and students of the faculty.
