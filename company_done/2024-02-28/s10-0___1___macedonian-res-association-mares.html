      About us

      MARES, supported by the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD), stands as a dynamic force, bringing together a diverse array of local and international investors deeply committed to propelling renewable energy initiatives in North Macedonia. Our vision is to shape a future where clean energy is not a choice but a way of life, creating a sustainable and eco-friendly energy landscape in the country. Our mission is to unite and empower all stakeholders within the renewable energy sector. We aim to facilitate a just and sustainable transition to clean energy, enhance the business environment, advance clean energy technologies, foster collaboration between public and private sectors to drive the transition to a greener energy landscape.

        It was a pleasure to contribute to the launch of the Macedonian Association of Renewable Energy Sources (MARES)! EIB Global | BEI Monde is working with the financial sector to help #decarbonising the #economy and #greening the business models of companies in #NorthMacedonia. Any effective market intervention will have to address both (i) #SME #financing constraints in the financial sector and (ii) bottlenecks in managerial #awareness and #capacity of firms. And this is what the European Investment Bank (EIB) is currently trying to achieve in North Macedonia in three complementary ways: 1. Firstly, we are providing FINANCIAL SUPPORT - in the form of credit lines or guarantees - to commercial banks, in order to make it more attractive for banks to allocate their capital to green projects. 2. Secondly, and linked to our financial support to banks, the EIB is putting in TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE, CAPACITY BUILDING & AWARENESS RAISING related to green finance. This support will be available to our local banking partners and Macedonian companies that consider green investments. 3. Thirdly, EIB also promotes and advises on POLICIES & REGULATIONS that support green finance, creating a conducive environment for commercial banks to engage in sustainable lending and investment. In North Macedonia, we will put our focus on the topic of climate risk management, both supervisory/regulatory level and when it comes to climate risk decision making and reporting at the level of commercial banks. Through these activities, we hope to ultimately support de-risking of the underlying project portfolios, and steering portfolio development activities towards greener and more climate-resilient #investments across the country. #EUwithYOU

        It was great to be back in Skopje this week for the launch of the Macedonian Association of Renewable Energy Sources (MARES), established with #technicalassistance from EBRD. These associations play a much needed coordination role between the government and private sector, so great job Endrita Xhaferaj, Violeta Nikolova, MBA, Francesco Corbo, and Stefan Kostovski for getting this off the ground. Thanks for having me to moderate the first panel - Strategic Opportunities of the Green Transition. NM has ambitious #decarbonisation plans, which will require a significant amount of new #renewables capacity to come online by 2030. To facilitate this, the country now needs to build on its previously successful RES tenders by introducing a two-way Contract-for-Difference (CfD) scheme and a clear schedule for new tenders. The CfD will give a stable revenue stream to investors, minimising the cost of capital (and hence level of support) while at the same time protecting the state (and hence consumers) from energy price spikes. It’s win win! The transparency associated with a well run tender is fundamental to protecting politicians from accusations of bias, which also supports bankability by reducing the risk of retroactive tariff changes - an absolute no no if you want to attract cheap capital! Again - win win!
