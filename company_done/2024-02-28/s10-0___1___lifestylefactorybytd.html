      About us

      LifeStyleFactory is based on the fundamental essentials of architecture, mixed with great passion for design and art and finding ways how to apply them in everyday objects and feed the passion for life through them. We have an experimental standpoint from which we work on small scale to large scale projects. The modern design is dominated by ideas relying on technicism and utilitarism, putting the comfort last. More attention is paid on pretty rather than on a good design. Term ‘pretty’ is a relative category that led to overcrowding with useless ordinary objects. They make people powerless against the uncontrolled blast of ideas and postulates on how the lifestyle’s image and core should look like.

                  Batu Caves, a Malaysian gem near Kuala Lumpur is guarded by a colossal golden statue of Hindu deity Lord Murugan, standing at 42.7 meters tall. These limestone caves harbour Hindu temples where dioramas of mythic scenes glow beneath stalactites, bats flutter in the shadows, and monkeys prey on tourists hiking the 272 stairs to Temple Cave. The temples, adorned with colorful sculptures and religious motifs, showcase traditional Dravidian architecture, reflecting the South Indian influence. Additionally, the annual Thaipusam festival at Batu Caves is a profound experience, where fervent Thamali worshippers display intense acts of penance, such as body piercings and elaborate kavadis, making it a vivid expression of religious dedication. www.lifestylefactory.co Photo credit: LifeStyleFactory All rights reserved: LifeStyleFactory

                      The LifeStyleFactory Team is traveling around the Globe looking for the most interesting architectural marvels and Singapore is definitely a must visit when it comes to architecture. Singapore is like the prestigious album of all the great names of Architecture. Today we are presenting you a very interesting concept when it comes to public housing. The Pinnacle@Duxton in Singapore stands as an architectural marvel, representing a paradigm shift in public housing design. Completed in 2009, its seven interconnected towers boast a sleek and modern aesthetic, redefining the city skyline. Designed by Arc Studio Architecture + Urbanism, the Pinnacle incorporates innovative features like sky bridges and sky gardens on multiple levels. These not only enhance the visual appeal but also promote communal spaces and sustainable living. The geometrically striking towers, standing as the world's tallest public housing project, reflect a meticulous blend of form and function. The Pinnacle@Duxton remains a testament to Singapore's commitment to pioneering urban architecture, setting a precedent for high-density residential developments that prioritize aesthetics, green spaces, and a sense of community.
